About the Center

The Lady Bird Johnson Wildflower Center is a nationally acclaimed research center and botanic garden that showcases and promotes the use of native plants. Founded in 1982 by Lady Bird Johnson, the Center’s vision and mission is to create a world enriched and supported through our interactions with the land and to conserve, restore and create healthy landscapes using our extensive knowledge of native plants and native ecosystems.

The Center is located on 279 acres in Southwest Austin in the Edward’s Plateau ecoregion. It has approximately 11,000 members, 60 employees, and 850 volunteers and is visited by more than 100,000 people annually. A 45-member advisory council oversees the Center’s activities.

Though part of UT Austin, the Center is self-supporting, with a $10.5 million endowment and $5.7 million annual operating budget that comes from visitor-related revenues and fundraising. It has expanded its national reach through a range of activities, which include the research and consulting of its Ecosystem Design Group, the Sustainable Sites Initiative, a vibrant Education Program, the Plant Conservation Program, and the web-based North American native plant database.

The Center is currently jointly administered by the School of Architecture and the College of Natural Sciences.

A complete description of all programs and more detailed history on the Center’s website at www.wildflower.org. Explore the Center’s latest annual report.

About the Executive Director Position

The Wildflower Center and University are looking for a dynamic individual to provide visionary leadership.

The Executive Director should be committed to providing leadership for the continued development of nationally recognized programs of instruction, research, and public service, and for guiding the Center in its fundraising efforts and interactions with external constituents. The Executive Director is expected to play a major role in the generation of income for the Center.

The Executive Director must have a professionally recognized reputation based on research, scholarship, and/or creative practice with experience in natural resources conservation, landscape architecture, planning, ecology, environmental sciences, horticulture, botany or a related field. Administrative excellence demonstrating leadership, personnel management skills, and expertise in communicating in culturally diverse environments is required, as is experience securing financial support for research, capital projects and programs. An advanced degree is required.
